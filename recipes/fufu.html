Fufu with Egusi Soup

Description

Fufu is atple food commonly eaten in West Africa. Although it originated from West Africa. It has become a sensation on Tiktok after being tried by several people across the world.
It is made from a fermented cassava purée or yucca root, pounded with water into a soft, sticky dough.
It is served with soup like egusi, Okra, groundnut soup. Each of these soup can also be combined with Efo (vegetables leaves like Uwugu, sawa sawa, bitter leaves, tete and soko)

Ingredients

  • Fermented Cassava (also known as yuca) purée
  • Water
  • Blended Egusi (also known as White melon seeds)
  • dried fish
  • Ugwu (pumpkin) leaves
  • Chicken or meat
  • Mushroom (for vegetarians)
  • Onions
  • Pepper mix
  • Palm Oil
  • locust beans
  • salt
  • seasoning

Steps

  1. Add the cassava purée and ½ cup water to a medium pot and use your hands to break up any lumps, then stir with a wooden spoon until smooth
  2. Turn the heat to medium-high and cook for about 15 minutes, stir continuously to break up any lumps that form and adding up to ½ cup more water as needed to ensure the fufu is not stiff or lumpy, until it turns from bright white to off-white in color and the fufu is stretchy and smooth
  3. Remove the pot from the heat.
  4. Scoop 1 cup of fufu from the pot with a wooden spoon and, using wet hands to prevent sticking, roll into a ball or your desired shape.
  5. Repeat with the remaining fufu
  6. Place a pot on medium heat, add palm oil and heat for about 3 minutes (Do not bleach oil) add the reserved chopped onions
  7. Add the egusi paste in bits to pepper, reduce the heat, do not stir and cover the pot with a lid. Cook for another 10 minutes.
  8. After 10 minutes, gently break the lumps that has formed in the egusi into a desired texture
  9. Add your fish and meat or mushrooms at this point
  10. Add your washed vegetables at this point and stir to combine. leave to cook for 3-5 minutes to cook
  11. Ready to serve and eat
